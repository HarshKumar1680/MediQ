Title: Breast Cancer - Treatment and Oncological Management
Source: Based on Wikipedia, NCI, NCCN, ASCO Guidelines
Disease: Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide and a leading cause of cancer death. It arises from the epithelial cells of the breast ducts or lobules. Key prognostic and predictive factors include tumor stage, grade, hormone receptor (ER/PR) status, HER2 status, and molecular subtype.

MOLECULAR SUBTYPES
Luminal A: ER+/PR+, HER2-, low Ki-67. Best prognosis, endocrine therapy-sensitive.
Luminal B: ER+/PR+, HER2+ or HER2- with high Ki-67. Intermediate prognosis.
HER2-enriched: ER-/PR-, HER2+. Treated with anti-HER2 therapy.
Triple-negative (TNBC): ER-/PR-/HER2-. Most aggressive, no hormone or HER2-targeted therapy, chemotherapy mainstay.

TREATMENT APPROACHES

Surgery:
Breast-conserving surgery (lumpectomy/wide local excision) removes the tumor with a margin of healthy tissue, followed by radiotherapy. It provides equivalent survival to mastectomy for most early-stage patients.
Total mastectomy removes the entire breast. Skin-sparing, nipple-sparing, and contralateral prophylactic mastectomy options are available.
Sentinel lymph node biopsy evaluates axillary lymph node involvement with less morbidity than axillary dissection.
Breast reconstruction with implants, tissue expanders, or autologous flaps (TRAM, DIEP, latissimus dorsi) may follow mastectomy.

Radiotherapy:
Adjuvant radiotherapy after breast-conserving surgery reduces local recurrence. Post-mastectomy radiotherapy is given in high-risk cases. Hypofractionated and accelerated partial breast irradiation regimens have reduced treatment burden.

Systemic Therapy:

Hormone Receptor-Positive (HR+) Breast Cancer:
Endocrine therapy is the backbone of HR+ breast cancer treatment.
Premenopausal women: tamoxifen for 5 to 10 years. Ovarian function suppression (GnRH agonist: goserelin, leuprolide) plus tamoxifen or aromatase inhibitor for high-risk patients.
Postmenopausal women: aromatase inhibitors (anastrozole, letrozole, exemestane) are superior to tamoxifen. Sequential tamoxifen then aromatase inhibitor is an option.
CDK4/6 inhibitors (palbociclib, ribociclib, abemaciclib) combined with aromatase inhibitors significantly improve outcomes in metastatic HR+ HER2- breast cancer and in adjuvant setting for high-risk early breast cancer.
PI3K inhibitors (alpelisib for PIK3CA-mutant) and HDAC inhibitors (tucatinib combinations) are used in metastatic disease.

HER2-Positive Breast Cancer:
Trastuzumab (Herceptin) plus pertuzumab dual HER2 blockade with chemotherapy followed by 12 months adjuvant trastuzumab is standard for HER2+ early breast cancer.
Neratinib (extended adjuvant) for HR+/HER2+ high-risk patients.
Trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1) is an antibody-drug conjugate used for residual disease after neoadjuvant therapy.
Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) is a next-generation ADC with transformative efficacy in metastatic HER2+ breast cancer and HER2-low disease.
Tucatinib plus trastuzumab plus capecitabine for HER2+ metastatic breast cancer including brain metastases.
Lapatinib and neratinib are small-molecule HER2 tyrosine kinase inhibitors.

Triple-Negative Breast Cancer:
Chemotherapy regimens including anthracyclines (doxorubicin, epirubicin) and taxanes (paclitaxel, docetaxel) are standard.
Immunotherapy with pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1) combined with chemotherapy improves outcomes in PD-L1-positive TNBC.
PARP inhibitors (olaparib, talazoparib) for BRCA1/2-mutated HER2-negative breast cancer.
Sacituzumab govitecan (TROP-2 targeted ADC) for metastatic TNBC.

Neoadjuvant Therapy:
Pre-operative systemic therapy (chemotherapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy) reduces tumor size, allows breast conservation, and provides in-vivo assessment of treatment sensitivity. Pathological complete response predicts better long-term outcomes.

BONE HEALTH AND SUPPORTIVE CARE
Bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid) and denosumab prevent skeletal-related events in metastatic bone disease and adjuvantly reduce bone metastasis risk and improve survival.
